12.7 Efficient

In this Section we introduce two popular and general paradigms for effecient model search. With the first apprach - referred to as boosting - we take a 'bottom-up' approach to fine tuning the proper amount of capacity a model needs. That is, we begin with a low capacity (and likely underfitting) model, and then gradually increase its capacity by adding additional units (from the samm family of universal approximators) until we achieve a model that minimizes validation error. With the second approac - called regularization - takes the opposite 'top-down' approach. We begin with a high capacity (likely overfitting) model, and then gradually decrease its capacity (using a number of different strategies) until we achieve a model that minimizes validation error.
